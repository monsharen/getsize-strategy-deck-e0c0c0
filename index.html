02 Root cause

The real footprint is 10× bigger, and ~85% is thin.

We ask Google to index ~20,700 URLs across five families. Only ~3,200 carry a genuinely unique per-model chart. The rest restate the brand's generic chart, the exact pattern the core update demotes.

03 Why this sinks us

Google now scores quality across the whole site.

  • Mass-generated, near-identical pages are the exact target of Google's core updates and its "scaled content" spam policy.
  • Quality is judged sitewide. ~17,500 thin pages do not just fail on their own, they drag down our good pages too.
  • That is why Gant and Zara are stuck at position 7 instead of top 3, even though their pages are genuinely strong.
What Google sees today
A thin-page farm: ~20,700 URLs, ~85% near-duplicate, wrapped around a few good pages.

The few good pages cannot outrun the dead weight attached to them.

04 Under the hood · two systems

Two systems doing the same job.

We generate size content two ways, and they compete. One crawls each brand's real per-product chart. The other restates the brand's generic chart under a model name. The second is the layer Google demoted.

System A · crawler product pages (the asset)
crawler→ shoe_variation→ /en/shoes/{slug}
  • Backed by ~1.3M crawled size rows. 2,964 pages carry the brand's real per-product chart.
  • This is our defensible uniqueness, and this family held its rankings.
  • ~12,500 still fall back to a shared chart: a thinner tail to enrich, not delete.

06 The new strategy

Stop selling page count. Sell authority and a tool.

How Google should see us
GetSize is the authoritative source for official, brand-verified shoe size charts and cross-brand size conversion.

Two things competitors cannot copy back: we link every chart to the brand's own official source (trust), and we recommend a size from the shoes you already own (utility).

Structure the site as hub-and-spoke:

Front door

Homepage + ~130 brand hubs

Our biggest engine and highest-volume terms. Made best-in-class, aimed at top 3.

Spokes

~2,964 product pages with crawled charts + 213 real editorial pages

Kept and enriched. Each carries a genuinely unique per-model chart.

Prune

1,854 thin editorial pages (+ assess ~12,500 thin product pages)

Folded into their brand hub, then noindexed or redirected. No longer separate index entries.

Moat

Cross-brand size-conversion tool

Real interactive utility Google cannot call "thin", and the thing that earns links.

07 Five moves

What we actually do.

01

Fix the index footprint

Fold the 1,854 thin editorial pages first (Adidas + Puma), then assess the ~12,500 thin product pages. Index shrinks from ~20,700 toward ~3,200 pages that earn their place. Staged and reversible.

02

Make brand pages best-in-class

Unique content per brand, prominent size tool, click-tuned titles, FAQ and table schema. Target top 3 for "[brand] size chart".

03

Win the 213 real model pages

Enrich with the first-hand data Google rewards: community fit and conflicting-official-chart callouts.

04

Build the moat

Ship the cross-brand size-conversion tool that answers real queries like "zara size 38 in US" and "haaland shoe size EU".

05

Rebuild authority signals

Clean sitemap, hub-centred internal links, weekly recovery monitoring in Search Console.

08 Under the hood · new

How the new system decides what to index.

Every page has to earn its slot in the index. One rule decides. Everything that fails is folded into its brand hub instead of standing alone as a near-duplicate.

The index decision
fun indexable(page) =
   page.hasModelSpecificChart   // crawled or editorial
   || page.sizeModifier != 0
   || page.brand in [Converse, Allbirds]
   || page.hasCommunityFit   // the growth lever

// ~3,200 pass  -> index + enrich
// ~17,500 fail -> fold + noindex/301
  • Failing pages get robots: noindex and leave the sitemap, or 301 to the hub.
  • Their content becomes an anchored section on the brand hub; rel=canonical points there.
  • The redirectToSlug path already in the controller extends from duplicates to thin pages.
Where the equity flows
~17,500 thin URLs→ brand hub #section
~3,200 real URLs→ indexed & enriched
  • Hubs gain FAQPage and size-chart Table schema, one canonical per brand.
  • The size-conversion tool: server-rendered result at a stable URL, real interactivity, not template-thin.
  • Sitemap becomes indexable-only, so crawl budget concentrates on ~3,200 strong pages.
Roadmap

The rule is a starting point, not a ceiling. As community feedback grows, a model where real owners report a different fit earns a distinct community fit component on its page, separate from the size chart and showing what actual owners wear. That unique content lets the model re-enter the index on its own merit. Pruned pages are not deleted, they are candidates to earn their way back.

10 The honest trade

What we give up, and what to avoid.

We start by folding the 1,854 thin editorial pages, then assess the ~12,500 thin product pages carefully, since that family still holds rankings. The pages we prune convert at nearly 0% and are the anchor dragging the domain, so we trade near-zero real clicks for a shot at sitewide recovery. Standard post-core-update playbook.

The route to avoid
  • Generating more model or product pages.
  • Shipping the duplicate-only cleanup and hoping.

Both keep the thin-content signal in place and prolong the decline.

A Appendix · levers under evaluation

Other levers we are weighing.

Not part of the committed plan. Each is shown with its potential effect and what would have to be true to make it help rather than hurt.

Product-specific editorial content

Effect

Turns thin pages unique, captures long-tail fit queries, lifts CTR, helps sitewide quality.

Needs

A real content source. We have almost none (55 descriptions for ~15,500 pages), so it must be data-backed and piloted, not written blind.

Risk

Templated filler at scale is scaled content, and would deepen the penalty.

Data-backed fit signals

Effect

Unique, unscrapeable first-hand content (owners wear EU X, recommendation-log trends): exactly what the update rewards.

Needs

More per-model community volume (thin today). Surface size-recommendation logs and shoe-rack data as on-page facts. Compounds over quarters.

Cross-brand comparison pages

Effect

Captures a whole query class we do not cover ("X vs Y sizing"). Our size engine can generate accurate comparisons.

Needs

Generated from real chart data, not prose, with a strict quality bar. Start with high-demand brand pairs.

International / locale expansion

Effect

Large impression pools at low CTR in DE, FR and NL point to non-English demand we under-serve.

Needs

Quality translation and correct hreflang/canonical (fix the localized product pages first), not machine-translated boilerplate.

De-risk rule

Any of these gets a controlled pilot first: ~150 pages, a held-out control set, a 4-8 week GSC read, before any scaled rollout. We never push unproven content to thousands of pages.
